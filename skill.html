It was finalized to prepare for the outbreak of the editorial revolution on March 23, 1954 with the birth of the Revolutionary Committee for unity and work and the publication of the magazine "National" and on June 17, 1954 the committee of twenty-two (22) held a meeting at the House of Elias Driesh in Medina district in Algiers, where it was decided to blow up the editorial revolution. On October 23, 1954, the committee of six (06) held a meeting in the Rais Hamidou District of Algeria to expand the tasks and determine the tasks and on November 01, 1954 it was decided to start the editorial revolution with the appeal of November 01, 1954, where the appeal called for the independence of Algeria, the restoration of national sovereignty and the establishment of a social democratic state within the framework of Islamic principles. The National Liberation Front and its military wing, the National Liberation Army, were established. The first task of the front is to contact all the political currents that make up the national movement in order to urge them to join the March of the revolution, and to recruit the masses for the decisive battle against the French colonizer determine the date of the outbreak of the liberation revolution : the choice of Sunday night to Monday the first November 1954 as the date of the start of the armed action was subject to tactical - military data, including the presence of a large number of soldiers and officers of the occupation army on the weekend followed by their preoccupation with celebrating a Christian holiday, and the need to introduce the factor of surprise. Determine the map of the regions, assign their commanders once and for all, and put the last touches to the map of the offensive scheme on the night of the first of November. All this after the Algerian people knew that the French colonizer was not interested in political resistance but the use of force and that the liberation of Algeria was not impossible.
